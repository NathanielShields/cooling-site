The Science

Why Palms? Glabrous Skin & AVAs

Glabrous (hairless) skin on the palms contains specialized arteriovenous anastomoses (AVAs) ‚Äî direct arterio-venous shunts that can dissipate heat at >5√ó the rate of regular skin surfaces. This is the physiological mechanism behind the Stanford thermoregulation research and the basis for palmar vasocooling products like CoolMitt and CET CoreTx.

Our stations exploit this pathway with sustained, high-power thermoelectric cooling ‚Äî something battery-powered wearables cannot achieve. Unlike forearm water immersion troughs, the system is dry, hygienic, digitally controlled, and requires no water management.
