About Boreas

We are engineers and strategists who have lived and worked in extreme heat. Boreas was founded at Harvard to make evidence-based worker cooling accessible and affordable for the organizations that need it most.

The People Behind Boreas

Riva Kapoor
Co-Founder
Harvard Business School Cambridge University

Riva is a current MBA student at Harvard Business School. She studied Geography at Cambridge University with a focus on climate, and worked at the intersection of strategy and sustainability at L.E.K. Consulting, Shell, King Charles' Sustainable Markets Initiative, and Tesla. Growing up between India and the Middle East, where extreme heat is already a binding constraint on daily life, gave her a firsthand understanding of the problem Boreas is built to solve.

Nathaniel Shields
Co-Founder
Harvard Princeton University

Nathaniel is a master's student in engineering and business at Harvard. He previously worked as an engineer at VEIR, developing superconducting power transmission lines, where he designed all superconducting components, led the firm's ARPA-E (US Department of Energy) project, and was a major contributor to its technoeconomic model. He studied mechanical engineering and engineering physics at Princeton. Growing up on a farm, where he spent many a summer day working in the heat, has inspired his passion to help people work safely in extreme conditions.
