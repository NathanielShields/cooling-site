Best Practices & Existing Approaches

Evidence-based passive and behavioral cooling interventions. Each solution below includes real-world efficacy data, technical specifications, and environmental constraints, so your organization can deploy the right tools at the right price point.

Basic Cooling

Wearable Cooling Products

All efficacy data measured at WBGT ≈ 32°C, typical peak-hazard outdoor conditions. Performance degrades significantly at higher humidity (see constraints per product).

EVAP

Evaporative Cooling Towel

Wetted towel worn around neck / shoulders

~$15 / unit

A simple, highly accessible first-line intervention. Works by leveraging the latent heat of vaporization at the neck and shoulders, a high-blood-flow area, to draw heat away from core circulation. Best in dry climates; less effective as humidity rises.

Productivity Uplift at WBGT 32°C 3–8%
15–40 min Work returned / 8 hr shift
20–60 W/m² Local cooling power
0.5–3 hr Effect duration
↓ humid climates ✓ re-wet any water source PVA or textile
Core Temp Δ <0.1–0.3°C / 10 min (skin primarily)
Weight 0.05–0.2 kg (wet)
Durability 100s–1000s of uses (washable)
Recharge Every 30–180 min depending on humidity
Humidity Best in low RH; drops sharply at high RH
Temperature Range 0°C – 50°C ambient
Sources: AJP 2012 Lancet PH 2018 CSITE 2025
ICE

Wristbands / Pulse-Point Cooling

Gel or ice wristbands at forearm / pulse points

~$30

Conduction-based cooling targeting the high-vascularity wrist and forearm. Unlike evaporative towels, effectiveness does not depend on humidity, a key advantage in tropical climates. Practical wearable bands deliver 5–25 W cooling vs. the immersion upper bound of 120–200 W.

Productivity Uplift at WBGT 32°C 3–8%
15–40 min Work returned / shift
5–25 W Cooling power (both wrists)
30–90 min Duration per charge
✓ humidity-independent ⚠ freeze logistics required 2–6 swaps / shift
Core Temp Δ ~0.05–0.2°C / 10–20 min (field); up to 0.6°C in lab immersion
Weight 0.05–0.3 kg
Durability 100–500 freeze cycles (gel); 500–1,000 (PCM inserts)
Recharge 2–6 swaps per 8-hr shift; 30–120 min refreeze
Humidity Effective in both dry and humid heat (conduction)
Constraint Requires cold storage / freezer on site
Sources: Front. Physiol. 2023 J. Therm. Biol. 2020
FAN

Neck Fan

Battery-powered active convective cooling

~$65

Active airflow enhances evaporative cooling of sweat, improving perceptual comfort and allowing more efficient perspiration. Stronger effect in moderate-humidity heat; less effective in still, very humid air. Can be obstructive if full PPE is required.

Productivity Uplift at WBGT 32°C 3–10%
15–50 min Work returned / shift
20–80 W Local convective effect
2–8 hr Battery life
↓ very humid / still air ⚠ PPE compatibility Rechargeable USB
Core Temp Δ <0.2°C / 10–20 min; stronger perceptual benefit
Weight 0.1–0.4 kg
Durability 1–3 years (consumer); longer industrial-rated
Recharge Once per shift (or swap batteries); 30 min–2 hr charge
Humidity Moderate; reduced in high RH / still air
Noise May be obtrusive in some settings
Sources: Published airflow/evaporation studies; consumer product testing.
PCM

Phase-Change Material (PCM) Neck Tube

PCM cooling tube / collar worn at neck

~$35

PCM materials absorb heat as they melt, providing continuous steady-state cooling until fully liquefied. By selecting a PCM with melting point near skin comfort (20–25°C), sensory comfort is maintained while the material absorbs metabolic and environmental heat. Best long-duration passive option, at the cost of weight and cold-storage logistics.

Productivity Uplift at WBGT 32°C 6–15%
30–90 min Work returned / shift
20–80 W Active melting power
~3 hr Duration per charge
✓ humidity-independent ⚠ heavier than towels Rotate packs every 4–12 hr
Core Temp Δ 0.1–0.5°C / 10–30 min (depends on mass)
Weight 0.2–1.0 kg (depends on PCM mass)
Durability >500 melt/refreeze cycles
Recharge Refreeze 4–12 hr; employer rotates packs
Humidity Effective in dry or humid heat
Logistics Requires refrigeration or ice on site
Sources: Matter 2024
HEAD

Bandana / Neck Buff / Head Cover

Passive textile + solar radiation protection

~$10

The simplest active measure in any heat protocol. Head and neck covers reduce radiant heat load from direct solar exposure, an often-underestimated factor in outdoor WBGT. Damp buffs add modest evaporative benefit. Primarily a radiant load reduction tool, not a metabolic cooling tool.

Productivity Uplift at WBGT 32°C 1–6%
10–30 min Work returned / shift
5–30 W Net local effect
Continuous Duration (passive shade)
✓ negligible weight ◉ solar load reduction Mainly radiant, not metabolic
Core Temp Δ <0.1–0.3°C (mainly skin / radiant)
Weight <0.05 kg
Durability Washable; multi-year
Recharge Re-wet as needed (evaporative buff)
Humidity Radiant benefit any weather; evap benefit less in high RH
Sources: Sports Med Open 2022 Front. Physiol. 2015
Behavioral Interventions

Scheduling & Operational Controls

Low upfront cost interventions that shift when and how workers operate rather than cooling their bodies directly. We work with your business to balance operational needs with heat productivity, determining optimal timing for shifts, tasks, and hydration breaks.

SHIFT

Shift Timing Adjustment

Avoiding peak heat hours via split shifts or night shifts. Tailored to your site's diurnal temperature profile and operational constraints.

10–25%Productivity uplift on peak days
60–100 minPeak minutes recovered
Operational scheduling cost ◉ Depends on solar/diurnal profile

Source: BMC Public Health 2024

Rest, Shade & Hydration

Structured rest breaks in shaded areas with hydration stations. Shade canopies run hundreds to low-thousands of dollars. The foundation for any heat management program.
